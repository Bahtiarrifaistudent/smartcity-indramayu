Smart Governance in Indramayu

Voice Note: A brief explanation of the Smart Governance program in Indramayu by Bahtiar Rifai.

ðŸ’¡ Overview & Main Programs

Smart Governance in Indramayu focuses on transparency, public participation, and the digitalization of government services. The local government has launched several digital initiatives, including:

  • I-CETA (Indramayu Quick Response): a public complaint and service transparency platform.
  • SIMPANAYU: an online licensing system by DPMPTSP Indramayu.
  • Online Absence & Kerjaku: an attendance and productivity system for ASN/PPPK.
  • La-Da (Local Asset Tracking): a regional asset monitoring system to prevent misuse.

Despite many advances, there are still several challenges:

  • A decline in I-CETA usage suggests the need for increased public awareness.
  • The adoption of "All in One" public services is still under 1% of users.
  • Limited digital literacy and infrastructure in remote areas.
  • The need for integration between systems (licenses, complaints, performance) into one ecosystem.

Opportunities that can be developed include strengthening open data, citizen dashboards, and analytics-based monitoring.
